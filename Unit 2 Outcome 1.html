1.5: Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites:

With this being a blog site, it goes without saying that all information gleaned from other websites needs to be referenced, and this is what I have done to the best of my knowledge. There’s so much information out there and so many websites, one has to assume that information will be accessed randomly, downloaded as individual chunks, and used in different ways on other sites (must like I did with my blog posts). It is therefore important to include a copyright notice on as many individually deliverable items as possible (1).Website builders should put a copyright notice at the footer of their site, so should anyone copy something, the existence of the copyright notice will help to establish that the defendant had actual notice of your rights, and there will be little opportunity to claim ignorance (2)

1.6: Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using web pages and how we can overcome them:

Inclusivity is a big deal these days, meaning companies need to take people with disabilities. In the main this means websites need to be accessible for those with visual impairments. Text size and colour needs to be taken into account, along with the contrast between individual sections, making for easier reading (3). Colour blindness is also a major issue when it comes to the styling (use of CSS), esp since 4.5% of the population suffer from the condition (4). They mainly suffer with the lack of differentiation between colours, but the website, Designmantic (referenced below) has it that there are three different types of colour blindness (Protanopia, Deuteranopia, Tritanopia), dealing with red, green and blue deficiencies, respectively. This means companies designing sites need to be mindful of using certain combinations of colours.

1.7: Describe which are the appropriate file types for website:

There are many different types of file that can be used on the web, depending on the purpose. Some are very common (jpeg, gif and doc); others more esoteric. For a start nearly all websites start as HTML files, using CSS files to style them. Then we have the images that fit into these sites, the most commonly used being jpg, png and gif. Some images are “lossless” (gif and png), meaning they can be edited and saved countless amounts of times without compromising quality, whereas jpegs- although capable of detailing millions of colours at once- reduce in quality every time they are saved (5).
