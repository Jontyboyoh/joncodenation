About Me...

My name is Jon. I am 38 yrs old, and currently living in Whalley Range in South Manchester. FYI: it is on the Chorlton border, so I'm a bit posh, but not a lot because I'm originally from New Moston, where my family still reside. Indeed, I was actually born in Crumpsall Hospital (now North Manchester General), where someone has just died from the Coronavirus. I feel its best to start these things with an uplifting fact or two!. Of course, no one would have died back in the day, for there was more resistance back then. My mother used to promote the idea that a bit of dirt every day would lead to a healthier immune system, but soon backtracked on the notion once Social Services got involved. That's a joke... They've not caught her yet.

Background...

I assume background means "things I've done in the past in terms of work, hobbies and interests", so here goes...

My recent career history most comes from working in Social Care as Support Worker with both Mental Health and Learning Disabilities. I did this for the best part of eight years, and was even promoted to the position of Senior at one point in 2016. However, after losing my job for having a sense of humour (read: joking with the wrong people at the wrong time... I'm not bitter), I found myself unemployed. More about where this led later...

Before getting into the Social Care game, I had a series of sporadic jobs (Supply Teacher, Admin/Reception), interspersed with short-to-medium periods of claiming benefits. Suffice to say, my CV now looks like a patchwork quilt; each patch looking nice as a standalone, but rendered uncomfortable to the eye when viewed as a whole.

In a previous life (a term that makes me sound like a 70yr old man) I gained a degree in English Literature from the University of Sheffield back in 2003, and a Level 3 Diploma in Photography from The Manchester College in 2013. Between these dates, I also racked-up qualifications in Counselling (2008) and Teaching (2006)

This brings me nicely to my hobbies and interests... I like Writing and Photography. I am currently writing stand-up comedy sets, as well as what could be referred to as a memoir with a whimsical edge. The standup thing started when I did an 8-week course, culminating in a five minute set in front of 400 people at Trafford's Bowler's Exhibition Centre on December 20th 2019 (without the use of Valium). The set centred around me going on a hypothetical date with an imaginary girl from Chorlton, as a vehicle for taking a pop at all things spiritual and new age (a strange thing really, since I have benefitted from a lot of that stuff). Now I'm writing one about being unemployed, and will probably write another about an aesthete subjecting himself to the logic of learning computer code (heaven knows where the material will come from for that one!).

Code Nation... As in Coding... As in Computer Programming... Why? Just why?... I mean, I've always been prone to masochistic acts (in September last year I decided I wanted to go back into Teaching, which resulted in me crowd-controlling a set of kids with Learning Disabilities for seven hours a day), but I've never had myself down as a "techie" person. I mean, I'm the sort of person who calls my mate Roy (everyone has a go-to computer guy right?) to find out how to do really simple things, such as changing the brightness setting on my laptop, so how did I come to enrol on a Coding course?...

After picking-up a leaflet in the Job Centre whilst attending my first Universal Credit appointment, I called Code Nation, which led to a couple of telephone conversations with Ezra Veng-Christenson (Admissions Advisor), who invited me to an induction interview. Fear of programming aside, I was enamoured by the bean bags and big screens of the workspace, so jumped aboard. In the meantime, before starting the course, I had a chat with my other techie mate, Ed ("its basically boxes within boxes"), after which I needed professional counselling.
