Unit One

Outcome 3

3.1: Review the benefits and drawbacks of IT tools and systems used in terms of productivity and efficiency – could you have been more efficient when making your website? How? Could you have done it in teams?:

At risk of contradicting my previous answers, I think sometimes I just need to keep it simple. As much as things like Trello and Slack can improve productivity in a big business, it sometimes felt as if it was tech for tech’s sake, so maybe things like this do require a more human element. Breaking into teams or buddy partners based on competency level would have been a welcome addition to proceedings. When I say “competency level”, I mean someone who has a lot of coding/tech experience paired with someone with very little. Another drawback would be the fact that learning how to use these project management tools takes up valuable time that could have been spent learning code. But then I suppose this stuff comes as a package in such an environment.

3.2: Describe ways to improve productivity and efficiency:

Mindfulness and regular fresh air breaks might help me in the future. The yoga sessions on Tuesdays helped. I mean, this is the way it’s going now, isn’t it? Holistic. Wellbeing in the workplace. But certainly having some kind of peer review/buddy sessions would also be beneficial to me. Largely because, on a number of occasions during the delivery of JavaScript, I thought I was going to crack; my brain was being asked to do things it had never done before. So, nothing to do with the tech per ser (there’s a lot to say about digital check lists and communication apps in terms of pulling things together).

3.3 Develop solutions to improve own productivity using IT in digital projects (what would you do differently next time? Team work? Use of Slack?):

I got quite adept at checking-in with my Trello boards towards the end of the project, as I did with checking-in Slack to see what had been posted in there to aid me in my process (learning tools; extra assignments; tips and tricks), but I feel I could have done this from the off, as opposed to utilising it toward the end. Had I been doing a larger scale project I may have bought a new memory card for my uawei PSmart mobile phone, for- although I had the app for Slack- I would have liked to have downloaded the Trello app, but my phone ran out of space.

I really struggled with parts of JavaScript, so to repeat what I have mentioned above, I think team-work and peer review sessions/mentoring would have been a good idea… It might have also been a good idea for me to spend more time alone working on things I had more of an understanding of (to “crack-on” with the task), rejoining the “team” when I get stuck with something.

3.4: Describe how you would go about testing digital solutions:

Whilst building my website, at the same time as getting my head around CSS, I found myself constantly saving any changes made, then refreshing my HTML pages in the browser to see whether the change had been executed. I also followed the little “try it yourself” activities on the W3 Schools website, along with frequent research on various functions and terms on the web. This was a good pattern to get into, but not without its irritations for a perfectionist like me, for things kept going wrong or not looking the way I intended, which meant more checking and research. As the cliche goes, more often than not, it turns out I’d just had a bracket or a tag in the wrong place.
